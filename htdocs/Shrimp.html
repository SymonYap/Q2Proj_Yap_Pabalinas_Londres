Shrimps are located in all oceans and freshwater lakes and streams. They move through their environment by quickly pulling their abdomen in towards their body, creating a shooting motion through the water, and making them swim backward. They are easily distinguished by their semitransparent body flattened from side to side and their flexible abdomen terminating in a fanlike tail. They have a diet consisting of molds, diatoms, bacterial films, algae, and many more since they are the ones that filter the ocean.

In many countries, shrimp is considered a delicacy because it can be found in a large variety of recipes all over the world. Although very common shrimp contains many healthy nutritional benefits. Shrimps are a great source of protein despite being low in calories. It can be eaten raw or cooked. Shrimp meat is tender and firm and you can have a side of dipping sauce to dip with it because of how intact the meat is.
