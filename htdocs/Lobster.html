Lobsters are another one of the more popular crustaceans in the world. They also live in every single one of the world’s oceans just like crabs but must live in saltwater to regulate their body’s salinity. Their bodies have one big and muscular tail and five pairs of legs, in which three of those legs have claws. And, just like crabs, their foremost frontal claws are much larger than the others and have virtually the same uses for it as a crab does.

These crustaceans also serve yet another delicacy everywhere in the world, but by being boiled or steamed in the shell. The meat is very commonly paired with melted butter to complement the sweet taste of the lobster with the rich and creamy flavor of the butter. Such popular dishes include lobster rolls, bisque, lobster thermidor, and a lot more.
